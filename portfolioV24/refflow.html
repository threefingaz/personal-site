Refflow: A Tool to Streamline Sales Referrals Within a Large Financial Organization

Disclaimer: The project description is based on actual work experience. However, for confidentiality and legal reasons, all visuals and specific details included in this presentation are conceptual and have been modified. Any resemblance to actual events or living people is purely coincidental.

Role

I was responsible for designing both the mobile app and the desktop administration tool. I also proposed process improvements to enhance efficiency for the main users.

Project Scope

  • Mobile App: Tailored for salespeople, this app facilitates the making of sales referrals.
  • Desktop Administration Tool: Used to manage incoming referrals, including assignment to appropriate personnel, amendments to referrals, and access to analytics.

Team Collaboration

Development was led by a single developer. We engaged in regular catch-ups with business representatives to iteratively refine the app based on user feedback following the initial launch.

Mobile App Features

  • Referral Receipt and Management: Salespeople receive notifications of referrals for financial products they handle. They can then contact the referrer for more details.
  • Client Interaction: Once prepared, salespeople contact the client to discuss the referral.
  • Referral Closure: Successful referrals are linked in the CRM system to the corresponding deal, ensuring referrers receive their rewards.
  • Referral Tracking: The app tracks each referral's progress from receipt to closure, with stages marked as Acknowledged, Referrer Contacted, Client Contacted, and Closed.

Process Improvements

I recommended allowing salespeople to close referrals at any stage to enhance flexibility. Referrals are automatically marked as acknowledged upon opening to maintain process transparency. This adjustment reduces unnecessary friction and accommodates user behavior variability.

Sent Referrals

Salespeople can monitor the progress of sent referrals and view any assigned rewards.
